GOKULNATH

Valentine’s Day has a way of reducing romance to a checklist of flowers, a dinner reservation you forgot to make in time and a last-minute box of stale chocolate that expressed an apology more than affection. But chocolate, when chosen well, can be something far more interesting than a predictable heart-shaped gesture. Across India, a generation of makers is reworking the idea of what a chocolate gift can be. Think bean-to-bar pioneers working directly with cacao farmers in the south, pastry chefs turning couverture into jewel-like bonbons and small studios crafting bars that taste distinctly of place. Some focus on ethical sourcing and slow processing, while others lean into bold flavour, design or nostalgia. All of them make chocolate that feels considered enough to gift, and good enough to keep for yourself. Whether you’re leaning into the romance or just using February as an excuse to eat better chocolate, this guide gathers the homegrown brands reshaping India’s sweetest craft, one carefully wrapped bar at a time.
